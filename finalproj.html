DETECTION OF NEUROLOGICAL DISORDER THROUGH MACHINE LEARNING CLASSIFIERS

 Introduction:
 Neurological disorders like Alzheimer's disease impact millions globally, necessitating early detection for effective intervention. Additionally, sudden strokes, resulting from abnormal brain activity, cause significant mortality. Addressing these challenges, our project employs data analysis and machine learning techniques to assess the predictive accuracy of identifying potential disorders.

Project Objective:
 The primary goal of the project is to develop a predictive tool for neurological disorder detection, using data analysis and machine learning methodologies. Our focus is on assessing the accuracy of predictions rather than relying solely on brain images.

Dataset Utilization:
 We leverage the OASIS dataset, known for its rich neurological research data. This dataset contains a variety of attributes relevant to neurological disorders, allowing us to evaluate the predictive capabilities of different machine learning models.

Methodology:
 Through the utilization of Machine Learning, we create predictive models trained to assess the likelihood of a neurological disorder based on provided attributes. Our emphasis is on evaluating the accuracy of these models in making predictions.

Machine Learning Algorithms:
 We employ a comprehensive set of machine learning algorithms to accomplish our accuracy assessment. These include:

  • Decision Trees
  • Support Vector Machines (SVM)
  • Logistic Regression
  • AdaBoost
  • Random Forest
  • Extra Trees
  • Gradient Boosting
  • XGBoost
    •      Accuracy Assessment:
    The core objective of our project is to measure how accurately the models can predict the presence or absence of neurological disorders. We employ metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score to comprehensively assess the performance of our models.

    Application:
    Our models serve as a preliminary screening tool for healthcare professionals. By inputting patient attributes into the trained models, practitioners can gauge the likelihood of neurological disorders. Understanding the accuracy of these predictions can guide medical decisions regarding further diagnostics and treatment strategies.

    Conclusion:
    This project revolves around measuring the accuracy of predictive models for neurological disorder detection, utilizing a range of machine learning algorithms. While diverging from traditional brain image analysis, our commitment to improving early detection and healthcare outcomes remains resolute.

    Future Directions:
    In the future, refining our models and exploring additional datasets could lead to more accurate predictions. Collaboration with medical professionals and institutions can provide valuable insights for practical implementation.
